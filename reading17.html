Sincerity

When I met her, I was terrified of what my guilt meant. I was terrified by how much I relied on false identities and lies and cowardice. When I met her, something clicked, it wasn’t magic it wasn’t a light breeze sweeping up in its currents. It was slow and methodical and it was her, something about her earnestness. I wanted, for the first time, to be honest, to try and be sincere, as hard as that is. I’m so used to presenting an image, soft-spoken and vulnerable. Smart and reclusive. Visages I could take on and off but something about the simplicity of how she lived.

She inspired me, before i even realised it.
