Like a
Dream

i dont believe. i pray rarely, only when the need strikes me or when i feel the pull. i dont know what i did last night, if it were a prayer or a plea. but i thanked them, all of them, whichever one would accept it. i thanked them for allowing me to have you in my life. i thanked them for listening
because years ago, when i was young i remember hoping, begging, for someone with a soft smile and open arms. for someone with a voice like doves and eyes like the sun. its been years since then, the doves have lost their lusters and the sun only half as bright as it was when i was swimming in puddles. But i would recognize it with my eyes closed, the curve of your lips the lilt in your voice. i look at you and think of summers ago and summers to be, i look at you and think of a lonely past and hope for a less lonely future.
“dream come true” always seemed like an exaggeration, exacerbated for effect. sweet nothings whispered on drunken nights when the words mean so much but hold so little. its 3pm on a Tuesday, the world at my finger tips. and yet i cannot find a sentence that better describes what i feel. you are the mist of memories come together, clouds of dreams formed into a person. i think i have always loved her, i think i always will.
